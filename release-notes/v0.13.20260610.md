# FreePair v0.13.20260610

FreePair is a free, open-source desktop application for chess-tournament directors. It imports SwissSys 11 `.sjson` files, runs USCF Swiss and FIDE Dutch pairings (via FreePair's own USCF engine and the BBP Pairings FIDE engine, both bundled), and gives the TD end-to-end tools to pair, score, intervene, and publish reports.

This release is all about **running a multi-section event without fighting the screen**: pop any section view into its own window, click a player's name anywhere to edit them, and stay in control of seeding before round 1.

## What's new in v0.13

- **Open any section view in its own window.** Every section tab — Pairings, Standings, Wall Chart, Players, and the rest — now has an **"Open in window"** button that pops it out into a separate, resizable window. There are also one-click **"All pairings in windows"**, **"All standings in windows"**, and **"All wall charts in windows"** buttons that open one window per section at once, so you can line up every section's pairings across your monitors. The pop-out windows are **read-only mirrors** — they update live as you pair rounds and enter results in the main window, and they remember their size and position for next time.
- **Click a player's name anywhere to edit them.** Player names on the Pairings, Standings, and Wall Chart screens are now clickable and open the full player editor (including the requested-byes editor for future rounds) — the same dialog the Players tab uses. No more hunting back through the roster to fix a name or add a bye.
- **Stay in control of seeding before round 1.** When you pair the first round, FreePair now **asks whether to keep your current pairing numbers or re-sort by rating**, instead of silently re-sorting. If you hand-tuned the order of equal-rated or unrated players to match your own seeding, it stays put.
- **Reset pairing numbers on demand.** A new **"Restore Default Pairing Number"** button on the Players tab re-seeds everyone by rating (highest first), breaking ties alphabetically by name — handy after manual edits. It's available before round 1 only, and tells you clearly when pairing has already started and the numbers are locked.
- **Edit byes where you edit the player.** Requesting or changing a player's future-round byes now happens right inside the player editor, so identity and byes live in one place.
- **Move the full-point bye in the pairing preview.** When a round has an odd number of players, the full-point bye now shows as a chip you can **drag onto a different player** in the pairing-preview dialog — the player you drop it on takes the bye and the other returns to the board.
- **Clearer Players list.** The first two columns are now labelled **"Pair Number"** and **"USCF ID"**, and the seed up/down arrows sit at the front of the row next to the number they change.

## Known limitations (still planned for later releases)

- **Pop-out windows are read-only.** They're for viewing and arranging across monitors; all editing still happens in the main window (which the pop-outs mirror live). The popped pairings window shares its round selection with the main window.
- **Some discretionary colour and float choices still differ from SwissSys.** A handful of late-round situations turn on which player floats between score groups, or on a colour call SwissSys makes without a clear rule basis; FreePair follows the stricter USCF reading in those cases. These are validated against a large real-world SwissSys corpus and tracked as deliberate, documented trade-offs.
- **Cross-half colour interchange (USCF 29E5d) remains present but switched off**, pending a validated rule for when SwissSys takes an interchange versus tolerating a colour clash.
- **Quads are formed by rating order.** The one-click split seeds strictly by rating; there's no manual re-seeding of who lands in which quad yet.
