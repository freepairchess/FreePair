# FreePair v0.21

A FIDE Swiss robustness release. FreePair now pairs FIDE Swiss events
cleanly through situations that previously made the FIDE engine stop with
an error — players who withdraw partway through an event, and first-round
no-shows (forfeits). Pairings for events without these situations are
unchanged, and the USCF/SwissSys side is untouched.

## What's new in v0.21

- **Mid-event withdrawals no longer break FIDE pairings.** When a player
  who has already been paired in at least one round leaves the event, FreePair
  now keeps the rest of the field pairing normally for every remaining round.
  Previously a withdrawal in a later round could make the FIDE engine reject
  the round and stop; now the withdrawn player is simply set aside and
  everyone else is paired around the gap.

- **First-round no-shows (forfeits) are handled cleanly.** Events where a
  scheduled game isn't played — one player doesn't show and the result is
  recorded as a forfeit — now feed the FIDE engine correctly. Forfeits are
  recorded as the FIDE "unplayed game" they are, so later rounds pair without
  errors.

- **Withdraw vs. delete is now consistent.** A player who has ever been
  paired for a game — even a game that ended in a forfeit, or a round where
  they took a bye — can be **withdrawn** (kept in the event's history) but not
  silently deleted from the roster. A player who never got paired at all can
  still be removed outright. This keeps the crosstable and every later round
  internally consistent.

- **Broader real-world validation.** FreePair's FIDE Swiss pairings are now
  checked, round by round, against a large set of real, completed FIDE Swiss
  tournaments — including large opens, deep multi-round events, and events
  with byes, forfeits, and mid-event withdrawals. The overwhelming majority of
  rounds reproduce the published pairings exactly, colours included; the few
  that differ are documented discretionary choices, not errors.

## Known limitations (deliberate trade-offs, planned for later)

- **A handful of rounds in very large or unusual events still differ.** In a
  few events, a single round can pick a different — but equally legal — bye
  recipient or colour transposition than the original software did. These are
  isolated, don't repeat in later rounds, and never make two players meet
  twice; they're tracked as known discretionary differences.

- **Accelerated pairings aren't reproduced yet.** Some very large opens seed
  the first couple of rounds with "accelerated" pairings. FreePair pairs those
  events as standard Swiss for now; matching the accelerated method is planned.

- **Prior colour and board-ordering divergences from SwissSys** catalogued in
  earlier releases are unchanged; none change who plays whom or whether anyone
  meets twice.
