# FreePair v0.26

A tournament-operations release focused on **NA Chess Hub roster syncing** and
**getting byes right**. You can now sync every section against NA Chess Hub in
one click, FreePair correctly reads **both half-point and zero-point bye
requests** from your file, and a section's pairing style is now driven by what
you choose in FreePair rather than a website label.

## What's new in v0.26

- **Sync every section with NA Chess Hub at once.** Alongside the per-section
  "Sync with NACH" button, there's now a **"Sync all sections with NACH"**
  button on the event panel. It checks the whole event in one pass and shows
  every new player and bye-request change across all sections in a single
  review list (grouped by section), so you can apply them together. It only
  appears for events that carry NA Chess Hub credentials.

- **Bye requests are read correctly — including zero-point byes.** NA Chess
  Hub lets a player request more byes than the section's half-point
  allowance: the first byes are half-point, the rest are zero-point, written
  in the file as e.g. `2 3 5z 6z`. FreePair previously **dropped the
  zero-point (`z`) byes** when reading the file. Now it reads both kinds
  correctly, the pairing engine sits the right players out for the right
  rounds, and the **roster sync compares both half-point and zero-point bye
  requests** so a change to either is offered to you. (Already-played byes
  are treated as history and never re-applied.)

- **"In sync" is now confirmed, not silent.** When you press a Sync button and
  nothing has changed, FreePair now shows a brief "everything matches"
  confirmation so you know the check actually ran.

- **Your pairing choice drives the section — not a website label.** A section's
  type code in the file is a NA Chess Hub *event category* (Open, Closed,
  Scholastic, Invitational, …), which has nothing to do with how the section
  is paired. FreePair no longer reads pairing style from it: every section
  opens ready to pair as Swiss, and **you** decide if it becomes quads or a
  round robin. As a result, options that used to disappear on some events —
  most visibly **accelerated pairings** — now show up where they should.

- **Accelerated pairings + same-team avoidance are always offered.** These
  Swiss-pairing options are now available on any section. If you instead pair
  a section as **quads** or a **round robin**, FreePair warns you that those
  settings don't apply to that schedule and will be ignored — so nothing
  happens silently.

- **Pairing-screen and event-config polish.** The Pairings tab now keeps the
  "Calculating pairings…" indicator on screen while the engine works
  (including the first round); the pairing toggles (avoid same team, show team
  name, accelerate, check NACH) sit on their own toolbar row instead of
  trailing off to the side; and the Event-configuration screen groups the
  venue address and time zone with the title and dates to save space.

- **Download:** [FreePair-win-x64-Setup.exe](https://github.com/freepairchess/FreePair/releases/latest/download/FreePair-win-x64-Setup.exe)

- **Auto-update:** Existing FreePair installs (v0.3 and later) pick up v0.26
  automatically. To update now, click "Check for updates" in the Settings tab.

The installer bundles the .NET 10 runtime, the BBP Pairings FIDE engine, and
the rules-reference document — no additional downloads or configuration
required.

## Known limitations (deliberate trade-offs, planned for later)

- **The NA Chess Hub sync covers players and bye requests.** It compares the
  player list and both half-point and zero-point bye requests — the things
  that change during registration. It does not reconcile other fields
  (ratings, contact details), and it never removes a player automatically: a
  player you've entered locally who isn't on the website is flagged for you to
  decide, not deleted.

- **A handful of late-round colour and pairing choices remain at the TD's
  discretion.** Validated against a large real-world SwissSys corpus, FreePair
  reproduces the great majority of pairings exactly and matches 100% on many
  representative events. A small number of genuinely discretionary situations
  — where two pairings are equally valid under the rule book and SwissSys
  picks one by convention — are documented and left to the TD rather than
  forced to a single interpretation.
