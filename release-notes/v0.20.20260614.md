# FreePair v0.20

A round-robin release. FreePair can now pair a whole section as a
round-robin (all-play-all) in a single click — choosing the pairing table
and how players are seeded — and lets you clear and re-pair a round-robin
just as easily. Swiss events pair exactly as before; nothing about the
Swiss engine changed.

## What's new in v0.20

- **Pair a section as a round-robin, in place.** A new "Pair as
  Round-Robin" button sits next to "Pair as Quads" on the Pairings tab of a
  section that hasn't been paired yet. One click converts the section to a
  round-robin and pairs **every round at once**, the same way quads are
  paired immediately. The section keeps its name, players, and ratings —
  there are no extra sections to manage.

- **Choose the pairing table.** The setup dialog lets you pick the
  **Berger tables** — the standard tables from the FIDE handbook, the same
  ones used for rating- and title-norm round-robins — or **FreePair
  balanced**, which plays the same opponents but spreads everyone's white
  and black games as evenly as possible. Berger is the default, and it
  reproduces the published tables exactly, colours included.

- **Decide how players are seeded.** Seed numbers drive who meets whom and
  when, so the dialog gives you four options: keep the current order, sort
  by rating (high to low), shuffle at random (with a reshuffle button), or
  assign every seed by hand in a simple grid. Manual seeding is checked for
  you — each seat from 1 to N must be used exactly once.

- **Odd fields are handled and flagged up front.** If the section has an
  odd number of players, the dialog tells you before you commit: one player
  sits out each round on a full-point bye, the event runs one extra round,
  and every player gets exactly one bye. No surprises after pairing.

- **Clear a round-robin and start over.** Because a round-robin is paired
  all at once, a new "Delete all rounds" button clears the entire schedule
  in one step (with a confirmation), so you can re-pair the section — for
  example with a different table or seeding — without deleting rounds one at
  a time. Your players and their seeding are kept.

## Installer

- **Download:** [FreePair-win-x64-Setup.exe](https://github.com/freepairchess/FreePair/releases/latest/download/FreePair-win-x64-Setup.exe)
- **Portable zip:** [FreePair-win-x64-Portable.zip](https://github.com/freepairchess/FreePair/releases/latest/download/FreePair-win-x64-Portable.zip)
- **Auto-update:** Existing FreePair installs (v0.3 and later) pick up v0.20
  automatically. To update now, click "Check for updates" in the Settings tab.

The installer bundles the .NET 10 runtime, the BBP Pairings FIDE engine, and
the rules-reference document — no additional downloads or configuration
required.

## Known limitations (deliberate trade-offs, planned for later)

- **Round-robin format is single round-robin only.** Double round-robins
  (everyone plays everyone twice, with reversed colours) are not generated
  yet; the section is paired as a single all-play-all cycle.

- **The chosen table and seeding are session settings.** The pairing table
  and seeding method you pick drive the pairings that get saved, but the
  choices themselves aren't written back into the SwissSys file. Re-opening
  an already-paired round-robin shows the finished pairings, as expected.

- **Prior colour and board-ordering divergences from SwissSys** catalogued
  in earlier releases are unchanged; none change who plays whom or whether
  anyone meets twice.
