# FreePair v0.47

A **team & Bughouse events** release. FreePair now speaks NA Chess Hub's updated
team-event format, so team and Bughouse events created or downloaded from your
registration provider open and work directly. Nothing changes for ordinary
individual events — every non-team section pairs, scores, and reports exactly as
before — so upgrading is low-risk and recommended.

## What's new in v0.47

### Team & Bughouse events

- **FreePair understands team events now.** An event can be marked a **Team
  Event** (with a **Team Size**, e.g. 5 players per team) and, when only some of
  those players play each match, a separate **Match Size** (e.g. 4 boards per
  match, like a chess Olympiad). **Bughouse** is simply a team event with team
  and match size fixed at 2 — check the **Bughouse** box and FreePair sets the
  rest for you.
- **Open a team or Bughouse file straight from your registration provider.** If
  your event file already carries the team/Bughouse settings, FreePair reads them
  and lets you work with the event right away — including a pre-calculated
  Bughouse section, which you can pair directly.
- **Turn a regular roster into Bughouse in one click.** On a Bughouse section,
  **Make bughouse section** pairs each two-person team into a single competitor:
  the team name becomes the entrant, the two players' ratings are **averaged**
  for pairing, and both names are shown together. The result is a **Double Swiss**
  ready to pair.
- **Clear, guided setup.** Event Configuration shows the team settings on one
  tidy row (the Bughouse box only appears once Team Event is on; team/match size
  are hidden and fixed at 2 for Bughouse). Each section can be marked a team or
  Bughouse section on its Overview tab — except a section that's already a
  calculated team/Bughouse section, which shows a banner and keeps the actions
  that don't apply (re-making, syncing its roster online, verifying IDs, etc.)
  safely out of the way.

### Fixes and polish

- **The "starting board" prompt is back.** When you click **Pair Next Round**,
  FreePair again asks you to confirm or set the section's starting board number
  for that round (it had stopped appearing). You'll also see, on the disabled
  **Pair Next Round** item, a tooltip explaining *why* it's disabled — most often
  "This section has no scheduled rounds," pointing you to set the round count.
- **Recent-tournaments list is easier to read.** Wider dialog, full file names,
  and no empty trailing column.

rules-reference document — no additional downloads or configuration required.

## Known limitations

- **Team-event pairing is per-team for now.** FreePair pairs Bughouse teams as
  single competitors (Double Swiss). Full team pairing — pairing two teams and
  then assigning players board-by-board within the match — is planned for a
  future release; individual-board results are still recorded on your own score
  sheets.
- **SwissSys fidelity is unchanged.** The Swiss pairing engine and its regression
  coverage are exactly as in the previous version; the known divergences remain
  catalogued as deliberate future-work trade-offs.
