# FreePair v0.17.20260612

This release sharpens **pairing fidelity** in three places a tournament
director will actually notice, all driven by a side-by-side comparison
against SwissSys on a real multi-section event. Two are pairing-engine
improvements; one fixes a score that displayed wrong on the Pairings tab.
Upgrading is recommended for every TD — the pairings produced now agree
with SwissSys on strictly more situations than v0.16, with no event pairing
worse than before.

## What's new in v0.17

- **No more avoidable rematches.** In a handful of late-round situations
  the engine could be forced to pair two players who had *already met*,
  because it committed the top boards of a round before discovering the
  bottom boards had painted themselves into a corner. The engine now
  **repairs that automatically** — it rebalances the affected boards so
  nobody is paired twice, choosing the least-disruptive fix and keeping the
  natural rating order (the higher-rated player still meets the stronger
  opponent). Across the entire regression corpus the engine now produces
  **zero repeat pairings**, and a permanent test guards it so the situation
  can never quietly return. Avoiding a rematch is the single highest-priority
  USCF pairing rule (27A1), so this is the most important fix in the release.

- **Better colour balance when a player floats down.** When the top player
  of a score group must drop to play in the group below, the engine now
  **releases that player from their "play the strongest opponent" lock when
  doing so clears a materially worse set of colour assignments** — matching
  how SwissSys resolves the same situation. The result is fewer players
  pushed onto the wrong colour in exactly the rounds where colour pressure
  is highest. The change is deliberately conservative: it only overrides the
  "strongest opponent" preference when the colour improvement is substantial,
  so ordinary float-downs are untouched.

- **Bye rows on the Pairings tab now show the correct running score.** A
  player on a half-point bye was shown with their score *including* that
  bye's points (e.g. a 2.0 player on a half-point bye read "2.5"). The
  Board-0 bye row now shows the player's score **going into** the round —
  matching every regular board row, the pre-pairing preview, and SwissSys.
  The bye's own points are still shown in the result column, so nothing is
  lost; the running score is simply no longer double-counted.

## Known limitations (deliberate trade-offs, planned for later)

- **One colour-driven float-down choice still follows our rule rather than
  SwissSys's.** When a score group sends a player down and two candidates
  are nearly equal, SwissSys sometimes picks the one that produces a cleaner
  colour split on a single board where FreePair keeps the lowest-rated
  player. We validated against a large real-world SwissSys corpus that the
  obvious "always prefer the cleaner colour" rule **makes more events pair
  worse, not better** — so this one board-level difference is left as a
  deliberate, documented divergence pending a more precise rule. It affects
  only which of two similarly-rated players floats, never whether anyone
  meets twice.
- **A small number of board-ordering and colour-of-equal-claim differences**
  from SwissSys remain catalogued as future-work items; none change *who*
  plays *whom*, only colour or board number on isolated boards.
