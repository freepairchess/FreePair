# FreePair v0.29

A pairing-styles and printing release. **Double Swiss and Double Round Robin
now play each encounter as two games** — the same opponents meet twice with
the colours reversed — and you can **show or print both games of an encounter
together**.

## What's new in v0.29

- **Double Round Robin really plays everyone twice now.** Picking **Double
  Round Robin** schedules every pair of players for two games, once with each
  colour, instead of a single all-play-all cycle. When you set it up you choose
  the order: **two full cycles** (play the whole field, then replay it with
  colours reversed — the usual format), or **back-to-back** (each pair plays
  both of their games in two consecutive rounds before the field moves on). An
  odd field gives every player exactly two full-point byes.

- **Double Swiss pairs both games of a meeting at once.** For a **Double
  Swiss** section, clicking **Pair next round** now creates both games of the
  encounter together — game 1, then the return game with the colours flipped —
  so a meeting is always a matched pair. Enter both results before pairing the
  next encounter.

- **Clear round labels for two-game events.** Rounds in these events read
  **"Round 1 · Game 1 of 2"**, **"Round 2 · Game 2 of 2"** (back-to-back and
  Double Swiss) or **"Round 1 · Cycle 1"** / **"Round 4 · Cycle 2"** (two-cycle
  Double Round Robin), so it's always obvious which game of which meeting you're
  looking at.

- **Show or print both games of an encounter together.** For these two-game
  events, the printed pairings sheet and the **pop-out Pairings window** can
  show **both games of the selected encounter at once** — two labelled tables
  on one page, or two stacked blocks in one window. A **"Show both games of the
  encounter"** checkbox on the Pairings toolbar turns it on or off; it's on by
  default for two-game events and doesn't appear for anything else.

- **The printed score sheet now shows the result column by default.** The
  printed pairings PDF includes the **Result / Score** column out of the box, so
  the recorded result prints between the two players without your having to add
  the column in Advanced Options. If you've already customised your printed
  layout, your choices are kept.

- **Download:** [FreePair-win-x64-Setup.exe](https://github.com/freepairchess/FreePair/releases/latest/download/FreePair-win-x64-Setup.exe)
- **Portable zip:** [FreePair-win-x64-Portable.zip](https://github.com/freepairchess/FreePair/releases/latest/download/FreePair-win-x64-Portable.zip)
- **Auto-update:** Existing FreePair installs (v0.3 and later) pick up v0.29
  automatically. To update now, click "Check for updates" in the Settings tab.

The installer bundles the .NET 10 runtime, the BBP Pairings FIDE engine, and
the rules-reference document — no additional downloads or configuration
required.

## Known limitations (deliberate trade-offs, planned for later)

- **The "show both games" preference is per computer, not per event.** The
  toggle defaults on for two-game events; if you turn it off it stays off for
  the session. As with the other column and layout choices, it's an
  app-wide preference rather than something saved into the event file.

- **Column choices are app-wide preferences.** The columns you pick (and any
  custom names) apply across your events on this computer, not per-event — so a
  layout you set up for one tournament is ready for the next. This is true for
  the on-screen grid, the popped-out window, and the printed sheet alike.

- **A handful of late-round colour and pairing choices remain at the TD's
  discretion.** Validated against a large real-world SwissSys corpus, FreePair
  reproduces the great majority of pairings exactly and matches 100% on many
  representative events. A small number of genuinely discretionary situations
  — where two pairings are equally valid under the rule book and SwissSys picks
  one by convention — are documented and left to the TD rather than forced to a
  single interpretation.
