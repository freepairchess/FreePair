# FreePair v0.18

A focused pairing-fidelity release: the one colour-driven float-down choice
that v0.17 left as a known limitation is now **resolved**, so the engine
agrees with SwissSys on strictly more situations than v0.17 with no event
pairing worse than before. Upgrading is recommended for every TD.

## What's new in v0.18

- **Cleaner colour when a score group floats a player down.** When an
  odd-sized score group must send a player to the group below, FreePair
  previously always floated the lowest-rated player. In some rounds that
  puts the floated player onto a board where both players are due the same
  colour — a colour clash SwissSys avoids by floating a slightly
  higher-rated, colour-friendly player instead. The engine now makes that
  same colour-driven choice, but **only when it genuinely cleans up a strong
  colour clash on the board the floated player lands on** — the precise
  signal that separates the situations where SwissSys switches from the
  ones where it keeps the natural lowest-rated float. Ordinary float-downs
  are untouched, and the change never overrides the no-rematch rule: if the
  natural float's board is a repeat pairing, the engine leaves the existing
  rematch-repair in charge. This was the headline "known limitation" called
  out in the v0.17 notes; it is now closed.

## Known limitations (deliberate trade-offs, planned for later)

- **One colour-of-equal-claim difference on a single board remains.** When
  two players who have *no colour history yet* (both came in on byes) are
  paired in the same score group, SwissSys occasionally gives White to the
  lower-rated player where FreePair gives it to the higher-rated one. We
  validated against a large real-world SwissSys corpus that no simple rule
  (board position, rating gap, or first-round colour seed) reproduces
  SwissSys's choice without making other events pair worse — so this is left
  as a documented divergence pending a more precise model. It affects only
  the colour on an isolated board, never *who* plays *whom* or whether anyone
  meets twice.
- **A small number of board-ordering differences** from SwissSys remain
  catalogued as future-work items; none change who plays whom.
