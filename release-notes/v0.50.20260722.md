# FreePair v0.50

A **team-event polish** release that builds on the full team pairing added in
v0.48. Prizes now understand team sections, the Team Pairing view shows *why*
each pairing was made, and you can print the Team Roster and Team Pairing sheets.
Nothing changes for ordinary individual events, so upgrading is low-risk and
recommended.

## What's new in v0.50

### Team prizes

- **Prizes are awarded to teams.** In a team section, the Prizes tab now ranks
  **whole teams by match points** (the same order as the Team Standings) instead
  of individual players. Teams tied on match points **share** the prizes for the
  places they span — the cash is pooled and split evenly, exactly like tied
  individuals.
- **Clear team wording.** The winners grid shows the team and its match points,
  and a one-line note reminds you that prizes are on match points and how ties
  are shared. The printed prize sheet matches.

### See how each team pairing was made

- **Richer Team Pairing tab.** Each team in a match now shows its **rating**, the
  **match points it carried into the round**, and its **board-1 colour history**
  (e.g. `WBW`) next to the team name — just like the individual Pairings tab — so
  you can see at a glance the score group and colour balance a pairing came from.
- **Cleaner columns.** The tab reads Match · White Team · **Match Points** ·
  **Score** · Black Team · Result, separating the team match points from the
  board score.

### Print the team sheets

- **Print the Team Roster and Team Pairing tabs.** Both now have a **📄 Print**
  button that exports a clean landscape PDF with the usual event header and
  footer — the roster in seed order with each team's board line-up, and the
  selected round's team pairings. This joins the existing Team Standings and
  Team Wall Chart PDFs, so every team view now prints.

The installer bundles the .NET 10 runtime, the pairing engines, and the
rules-reference document — no additional downloads or configuration required.

## Known limitations

- **Team byes follow FIDE draw-equivalent scoring by default.** A team that
  receives a full-round bye is scored as a drawn match by default (you can switch
  to match-win in the team setup).
- **SwissSys fidelity is unchanged.** The Swiss pairing engine and its regression
  coverage are exactly as in the previous version; the known divergences remain
  catalogued as deliberate future-work trade-offs.
