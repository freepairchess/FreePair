# FreePair v0.24

A pairing-fidelity release. FreePair now matches the colours an experienced
TD (and SwissSys) would assign in a specific late-round situation: when a
player drops down a score group and the top board they'd normally take would
force two players to fight over the same colour, FreePair will quietly place
that player on a colour-friendlier board instead — provided doing so doesn't
cost anyone a pairing they'd otherwise get.

## What's new in v0.24

- **Smarter colours when a player floats down a score group.** In Swiss
  pairings, an odd score group sends its lowest player down to play the next
  group. Normally that player takes the top board of the lower group — but
  sometimes both players there are owed the same colour, which means one of
  them has to be denied. FreePair now recognises when shifting the dropped
  player onto a slightly different board lets **both** strong colour claims
  be honoured, leaving only a minor colour preference unmet instead. The
  result is the same set of opponents, paired with the colours a TD would
  pick by hand.

- **Only when it's strictly better.** This adjustment is deliberately
  conservative: it only triggers when it doesn't change *who* plays whom and
  doesn't create any new colour problem — it simply swaps a strong unmet
  colour claim for a milder one. Every pairing FreePair already got right is
  untouched; this purely improves the cases where a strong colour preference
  was being denied unnecessarily.

The installer bundles the .NET 10 runtime, the BBP Pairings FIDE engine, and
the rules-reference document — no additional downloads or configuration
required.

## Known limitations (deliberate trade-offs, planned for later)

- **A handful of late-round colour and pairing choices remain at the TD's
  discretion.** Validated against a large real-world SwissSys corpus,
  FreePair reproduces the great majority of pairings exactly and matches
  100% on many representative events. A small number of genuinely
  discretionary situations — where two pairings are equally valid under the
  rule book and SwissSys picks one by convention — are documented and left
  to the TD rather than forced to a single interpretation.
