# FreePair v0.12.20260609

FreePair is a free, open-source desktop application for chess-tournament directors. It imports SwissSys 11 `.sjson` files, runs USCF Swiss and FIDE Dutch pairings (via FreePair's own USCF engine and the BBP Pairings FIDE engine, both bundled), and gives the TD end-to-end tools to pair, score, intervene, and publish reports.

This release sharpens **colour pairing**: when the engine has to give one of two players a colour neither of them wants, it now makes the same choice an experienced TD — and SwissSys — would.

## What's new in v0.12

- **Smarter tie-breaking when two colour preferences collide.** Sometimes a score group can be paired more than one way and, whichever way you choose, exactly one board ends up with both players wanting the same colour. Earlier versions treated every such clash as equally costly and picked whichever arrangement disturbed the rating order least. FreePair now looks at **how strong each player's colour claim is** and prefers the pairing that disappoints the **milder** claim — keeping the strong claim (a player who is genuinely owed a colour to even out their whites and blacks) satisfied. This matches how SwissSys resolves the same situations.
- **More real events now pair identically to SwissSys.** This single refinement brought **two more independent real-world section-rounds into exact agreement** with SwissSys's published pairings, with **no change to any section that already matched**. If you cross-check FreePair against SwissSys, you'll see closer agreement on middle-and-late rounds where colour pressure builds up.

## Why this matters

Colour allocation is where good pairing programs earn their keep. In the rounds where several players are all "due" the same colour, the difference between a defensible pairing and an awkward one comes down to **which preference you choose to deny**. FreePair now denies the weakest claim available, so the players with the strongest colour case keep getting the colour they're owed — fewer awkward conversations at the board, and pairings that line up with what TDs running SwissSys already expect.

## Known limitations (still planned for later releases)

- **Some discretionary colour and float choices still differ from SwissSys.** A handful of late-round situations turn on which player floats between score groups, or on a colour call SwissSys makes without a clear rule basis; FreePair follows the stricter USCF reading in those cases. These are validated against a large real-world SwissSys corpus and tracked as deliberate, documented trade-offs.
- **Cross-half colour interchange (USCF 29E5d) remains present but switched off**, pending a validated rule for when SwissSys takes an interchange versus tolerating a colour clash.
- **`28L3` forfeit-win-bye exclusion** and **`28L4` "unless all others have already had a bye" escape** remain partial — small bye-picker refinements scoped as their own future branches.
- **Quads are formed by rating order.** The one-click split seeds strictly by rating; there's no manual re-seeding of who lands in which quad yet.
