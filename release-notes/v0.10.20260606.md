# FreePair v0.10.20260606

FreePair is a free, open-source desktop application for chess-tournament directors. It imports SwissSys 11 `.sjson` files, runs USCF Swiss and FIDE Dutch pairings (via FreePair's own USCF engine and the BBP Pairings FIDE engine, both bundled), and gives the TD end-to-end tools to pair, score, intervene, and publish reports.

This release fixes a **pairing-correctness bug** that could, in a specific situation, pair two players who had already met. If you run USCF Swiss events, this is a **recommended upgrade**.

## What's new in v0.10

- **Fixed: a downfloater could be paired against an opponent it had already played.** The "no two players meet twice" rule (USCF 27A1) is the single most important pairing rule — it ranks above colour and above every board-placement preference. A defect in the colour-balancing step could, when a player dropped down a score group to face the top player of the lower group, **re-create a pairing those two had already played** if that happened to balance colours slightly better. The engine now refuses to do this: a rematch is never accepted to improve colours. When the natural drop would be a rematch, the player is correctly slid to the next eligible opponent — exactly as the rule book (and SwissSys) require. This only ever affected the rare downfloat-into-a-prior-opponent case, but when it hit, it produced a clear rule violation, so the fix matters.
- **No change to any other pairing.** The fix is surgical: it only suppresses the would-be rematch and leaves every other pairing the engine already produced exactly as it was. Validated against the full regression corpus with zero change to any previously-correct section.

## Why this matters

A rematch in a Swiss event is the kind of mistake players and parents notice immediately and rarely forgive — two players who already met being told to play again. USCF rule 27A1 exists precisely to prevent it, and it overrides every lower-priority preference (colour balance, board order, even the "downfloater plays the strongest opponent" guideline). v0.10 makes the engine honour that priority without exception.

## Known limitations (still planned for later releases)

- **SwissSys fidelity is otherwise unchanged from v0.9.** The engine matches the curated regression corpus on the same set of sections; this release adds one correctness fix without shifting the broader fidelity numbers.
- **Cross-half colour interchange (USCF 29E5d) remains present but switched off**, pending a validated rule for when SwissSys takes an interchange versus tolerating a colour clash.
- **`28L3` forfeit-win-bye exclusion** and **`28L4` "unless all others have already had a bye" escape** remain partial — small bye-picker refinements scoped as their own future branches.
