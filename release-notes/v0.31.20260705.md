# FreePair v0.31

A tournament-desk workflow release. **Ratings are easier to keep current** — a
new one-click *Refresh Ratings* button re-pulls every player's live US Chess and
FIDE rating (with clear safeguards so you don't refresh at the wrong time). The
**section list is easier to read and navigate**, teammate avoidance now handles
the bottom of the standings the same careful way it handles the top, and a batch
of small display glitches are fixed.

## What's new in v0.31

- **Refresh a section's ratings in one click.** The Players tab has a new
  **Refresh Ratings** button that looks up every player's current **US Chess**
  and **FIDE** rating and drops each into the right column — no more editing
  ratings one player at a time before an event. FreePair only changes a rating
  when it finds a live one, so unrated players and anyone the services can't
  reach are left exactly as they were, and a summary afterward shows you exactly
  what changed and what didn't.

- **Refresh Ratings keeps you out of trouble.** Because refreshing at the wrong
  moment can quietly break an event, it's guarded three ways: it asks you to
  **verify the players' IDs first** (so it knows which rating goes in which
  column); if you've **already paired a round** it warns that changing ratings
  now can make the standings look inconsistent (and suggests deleting an
  unplayed first-round pairing instead); and it reminds you that an event which
  **started at the end of last month** should usually keep last month's ratings
  rather than pulling in a brand-new supplement. Each warning needs a tick to
  continue, so nothing changes by accident.

- **A cleaner, easier section list.** The list of sections down the left side is
  now its own titled panel with a running **section count**, each section's
  buttons sit neatly **beneath its name**, and **➕ Add section** lives right
  under the last section. Click the **"Sections"** heading to jump back to your
  sections — it reopens the one you were last working on.

- **Teammate avoidance now protects the tail of the standings too.** FreePair
  already avoids pairing teammates at the top of the standings unless it's truly
  unavoidable (so a prize fight isn't distorted). It now does the same at the
  **bottom** of the standings — matching SwissSys's "Plus 2 / Minus 2" behavior
  — so trailing teammates aren't pulled out of their group just to dodge each
  other. Teammates are still always kept apart whenever the group can be paired
  without them meeting; the on-screen help explains exactly when the exception
  applies.

- **Buttons and icons display correctly everywhere.** Some toolbar and action
  buttons were showing an empty box instead of their little picture icon; every
  button now shows its proper symbol. The copy-to-clipboard icons on the Players
  list were also tidied to the standard copy symbol.

- **The Players "Name" column resizes again.** You can now drag the Name column
  wider or narrower like every other column.

- **Clearer pairing-preview buttons.** The preview window's buttons now read
  simply **OK** and **Cancel**.

- **Download:** [FreePair-win-x64-Setup.exe](https://github.com/freepairchess/FreePair/releases/latest/download/FreePair-win-x64-Setup.exe)
- **Portable zip:** [FreePair-win-x64-Portable.zip](https://github.com/freepairchess/FreePair/releases/latest/download/FreePair-win-x64-Portable.zip)
- **Auto-update:** Existing FreePair installs (v0.3 and later) pick up v0.31
  automatically. To update now, click "Check for updates" in the Settings tab.

The installer bundles the .NET 10 runtime, the BBP Pairings FIDE engine, and
the rules-reference document — no additional downloads or configuration
required.

## Known limitations (deliberate trade-offs, planned for later)

- **Refresh Ratings needs to be online — and needs verified IDs first.** It
  reaches out to US Chess and NA Chess Hub, so it needs a working internet
  connection, and it asks you to verify the players' IDs beforehand so it knows
  which federation each ID belongs to. If a service can't be reached, that
  player's rating is simply left unchanged rather than guessed.

- **FIDE ratings come from a third-party service.** FIDE ratings are fetched
  from NA Chess Hub (US Chess doesn't provide them). If that service is
  unavailable, the US Chess rating still updates and the FIDE rating is left as
  it was.

- **Teammate avoidance is applied with the standard settings.** The top-and-
  bottom "accept an unavoidable teammate pairing" behavior uses the rule book's
  usual two-point cushion and is always applied together; per-side on/off
  switches and a custom cushion (as SwissSys offers) aren't exposed yet.

- **A handful of late-round colour and pairing choices remain at the TD's
  discretion.** Validated against a large real-world SwissSys corpus, FreePair
  reproduces the great majority of pairings exactly and matches 100% on many
  representative events. A small number of genuinely discretionary situations
  — where two pairings are equally valid under the rule book and SwissSys picks
  one by convention — are documented and left to the TD rather than forced to a
  single interpretation.
