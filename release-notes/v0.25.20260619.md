# FreePair v0.25

A tournament-operations release. FreePair can now **keep your roster in sync
with NA Chess Hub** while the event is running, and the **pairings — on screen
and on the printed wall sheet — now show each player's colour situation and
pairing number**, with a plain-language legend so players can read it.

## What's new in v0.25

- **Sync your roster with NA Chess Hub before you pair.** When you open an
  event from NA Chess Hub, players keep registering — and changing their
  half-point-bye requests — on the website, often after you've downloaded
  the file or even after a round is paired. FreePair can now check the live
  roster for you. Turn it on for the whole event or per section (it's off by
  default), and before each round FreePair quietly fetches the latest list,
  shows you exactly **who's new, who's missing, and which bye requests
  changed**, and lets you apply the changes one at a time or all at once.
  There's also a **"Sync with NACH"** button on the Players tab to check any
  time. It only ever offers to add players or adjust future bye requests —
  it never touches your pairings, results, or ratings, and it never blocks
  pairing.

- **Colours and pairing numbers on every pairing — and the wall sheet.**
  Each player now shows the **colours they've played** so far and the
  **colour they're due this round** (the familiar `w / W / WW` shorthand),
  alongside their **pairing number**, on both the Pairings tab and the
  printed pairings PDF. A **"Color Due Legend"** at the bottom explains the
  notation in plain English and points to the rule book your section's
  pairing engine follows, so a player can decode it without asking the TD.

- **Editable NA Chess Hub event link.** The NA Chess Hub Event ID on the
  Event-configuration screen is now editable, with a **"View on NACH"**
  button that opens the event's page in your browser — handy for linking a
  hand-built event to its online listing or enabling the roster sync.

- **Smaller workflow polish.** "Close tournament" now lives at the top of the
  toolbar when an event is open; the "Switch Player IDs" button only appears
  for events that actually have a second ID column; the pairing-preview
  screen now states avoid-team and accelerated-pairing status as plain
  "this round: On / Off" lines; and a couple of Event-config screen quirks
  (a stray selection after Apply, the Apply button greying out on hover) are
  fixed.

- **Two fixes worth calling out.** A player added from a NA Chess Hub sync
  now keeps their full ID, so a later bye change for that player is detected
  correctly on the next sync. And a player **added after the event has
  started** is now shown as active rather than being mislabelled
  "Withdrawn".

## Installer

- **Download:** [FreePair-win-x64-Setup.exe](https://github.com/freepairchess/FreePair/releases/latest/download/FreePair-win-x64-Setup.exe)
- **Portable zip:** [FreePair-win-x64-Portable.zip](https://github.com/freepairchess/FreePair/releases/latest/download/FreePair-win-x64-Portable.zip)
- **Auto-update:** Existing FreePair installs (v0.3 and later) pick up v0.25
  automatically. To update now, click "Check for updates" in the Settings tab.

The installer bundles the .NET 10 runtime, the BBP Pairings FIDE engine, and
the rules-reference document — no additional downloads or configuration
required.

## Known limitations (deliberate trade-offs, planned for later)

- **The NA Chess Hub sync covers players and half-point byes.** It compares
  the player list and half-point-bye requests — the things that change
  during registration. It does not reconcile other fields (ratings, contact
  details), and it never removes a player automatically: a player you've
  entered locally who isn't on the website is flagged for you to decide,
  not deleted.

- **A handful of late-round colour and pairing choices remain at the TD's
  discretion.** Validated against a large real-world SwissSys corpus,
  FreePair reproduces the great majority of pairings exactly and matches
  100% on many representative events. A small number of genuinely
  discretionary situations — where two pairings are equally valid under the
  rule book and SwissSys picks one by convention — are documented and left
  to the TD rather than forced to a single interpretation.
