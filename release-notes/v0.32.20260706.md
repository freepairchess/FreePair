# FreePair v0.32

A tournament-desk workflow release focused on **adding players in the middle of
an event**. When you sync with NA Chess Hub after play has started and a new
player has registered, FreePair now lets you decide — round by round — what to
record for the rounds they missed, instead of silently giving them nothing.

## What's new in v0.32

- **Late entries from a mid-event sync now get the byes you choose.** When you
  "Sync with NA Chess Hub" after one or more rounds are already paired and the
  sync finds a newly-registered player, FreePair opens a short **"Assign results
  for missed rounds"** step. For each round the player missed, you pick what to
  record — **half-point bye, zero-point bye, or full-point bye** — so a late
  arrival can start their first game with the right score instead of always
  starting from zero.

- **Sensible defaults, still fully under your control.** Each missed round is
  pre-filled for you: the player gets **half-point byes for the rounds they
  requested**, up to the event's half-point-bye allowance, and a **zero-point
  bye** for the rest. Any half-point byes they requested for *future* rounds are
  counted against the allowance first, so the total never exceeds what the event
  permits. Every round is a drop-down you can change before you accept, and one
  click applies the defaults for everyone at once.

- **Works for one section or the whole event.** Whether you sync a single
  section or use **"Sync all sections with NA Chess Hub"**, all the new mid-event
  players are gathered into the same step so you can set everyone's missed-round
  results in one pass. Players added *before* the first round is paired are
  unaffected — there are no missed rounds to assign.

- **Future bye requests still carry over as before.** A newly-added player's
  upcoming half-point and zero-point bye requests continue to come across from
  NA Chess Hub automatically; this release only adds control over the rounds
  that were *already* paired when they joined.

the rules-reference document — no additional downloads or configuration
required.

## Known limitations (deliberate trade-offs, planned for later)

- **The missed-round defaults follow the event's half-point-bye allowance.** If
  your event doesn't record a half-point-bye allowance, missed rounds default to
  zero-point byes (you can still change any of them by hand). This is the safe
  default — a late entry earns no points for a round they didn't play unless you
  say otherwise.

- **Adding a mid-event player still leaves the pairing to you.** As before,
  FreePair records the newcomer and their missed-round results; you then pair the
  next round normally. The new player carries no colour history from the byes, so
  their first game's colour is assigned like any other unpaired entrant.
