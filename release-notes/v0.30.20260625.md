# FreePair v0.30

A player-setup release. **Adding and editing players is faster and far more
accurate**: enter a US Chess or FIDE ID, click **Verify**, and FreePair fills in
the name, the player's other ID, ratings, and more — checked live against US
Chess (and FIDE ratings via NA Chess Hub). Plus the USCF rating report now
guards against filing results under the wrong ID, and several dialogs got a
polish.

## What's new in v0.30

- **Edit both the ID and the second ID (ID2) on a player.** The Add / Edit
  player window now has two ID boxes side by side. For dual-rated players you can
  keep their US Chess ID in one and their FIDE ID in the other, and a **⇄ Swap
  ID / ID2** button flips the two with a single click if you put them in the
  wrong boxes.

- **One-click Verify with auto-fill.** Each ID box has a **Verify** button that
  looks the player up live and **fills in the blanks for you** — name, the
  player's *other* ID, rating, US Chess membership expiration, state, and FIDE
  federation (into the Club field, the way SwissSys stores it). It only fills
  empty fields, so anything you've already typed is never overwritten. If you
  enter an ID before a name, FreePair adopts the looked-up name automatically.

- **FIDE ratings are filled in too.** US Chess doesn't publish FIDE ratings, so
  FreePair fetches them from NA Chess Hub. Verify a player's **FIDE ID** — or
  even just their **US Chess ID** (FreePair follows the link to their FIDE
  record) — and the standard **FIDE rating drops into the matching rating
  field**.

- **FreePair tells you if the IDs look swapped.** When your section is set up so
  one column is the FIDE ID and the other is the US Chess ID, each box shows what
  it expects ("Section expects a FIDE ID here"), and Verify warns you if the
  value looks like the wrong type — the cue to hit **Swap**. These checks are
  **advisory only**: whatever you enter, FreePair never stops you from adding the
  player or pairing.

- **The USCF rating report won't file results under the wrong ID.** When you
  export the US Chess report, FreePair now writes each player's **US Chess** member
  ID — even if you've put the US Chess ID in the second column and the FIDE ID
  first. Before writing, it checks every player and warns you about anyone whose
  ID is missing, looks like a FIDE ID in the US Chess field, or can't be told
  apart — so you can **cancel and fix it, or export anyway**.

- **"Browse online events" remembers your filter.** The event browser keeps the
  text you typed last time, shows **how many of the total events** the filter is
  showing (so you don't mistake a filtered list for "all there is"), and adds a
  **Clear Filter** button. The window is also a bit wider.

- **Tidy up your "Open recent" list.** Each recent tournament now has a
  **Remove** button, plus **Remove all** to clear the whole list — neither
  deletes any files. Tournaments whose file has been moved or deleted show with a
  **line through them** so you can spot and remove dead links at a glance.

- **Buttons look better everywhere.** Button labels across the app's dialogs are
  now centered with comfortable spacing instead of hugging the left edge.

- **Download:** [FreePair-win-x64-Setup.exe](https://github.com/freepairchess/FreePair/releases/latest/download/FreePair-win-x64-Setup.exe)
- **Portable zip:** [FreePair-win-x64-Portable.zip](https://github.com/freepairchess/FreePair/releases/latest/download/FreePair-win-x64-Portable.zip)
- **Auto-update:** Existing FreePair installs (v0.3 and later) pick up v0.30
  automatically. To update now, click "Check for updates" in the Settings tab.

The installer bundles the .NET 10 runtime, the BBP Pairings FIDE engine, and
the rules-reference document — no additional downloads or configuration
required.

## Known limitations (deliberate trade-offs, planned for later)

- **ID verification needs to be online.** Verify and the auto-fill it powers
  reach out to US Chess and NA Chess Hub, so they need a working internet
  connection. If a lookup can't reach a service it simply reports that it
  couldn't verify and fills nothing — it never blocks you, and you can always
  type the details in by hand.

- **FIDE ratings come from a third-party service.** FIDE ratings are fetched
  from NA Chess Hub (US Chess doesn't provide them). If that service is
  unavailable, the US Chess details still fill in and the FIDE rating is simply
  left for you to enter.

- **The remembered event filter and "Open recent" list are per computer.** Like
  FreePair's other display preferences, these are saved on the computer you're
  using rather than inside any event file.

- **A handful of late-round colour and pairing choices remain at the TD's
  discretion.** Validated against a large real-world SwissSys corpus, FreePair
  reproduces the great majority of pairings exactly and matches 100% on many
  representative events. A small number of genuinely discretionary situations
  — where two pairings are equally valid under the rule book and SwissSys picks
  one by convention — are documented and left to the TD rather than forced to a
  single interpretation.
