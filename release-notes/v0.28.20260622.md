# FreePair v0.28

A pairing-engine and usability release. The section **pairing style now drives
the actual pairings**, not just the buttons — so what you see and what FreePair
pairs always agree — and the **popped-out Pairings window** now matches your
on-screen grid instead of the printed score sheet.

## What's new in v0.28

- **Your pairing style now drives the engine, not just the buttons.** In v0.27
  a section's **Pairing rule** (Swiss, Round Robin, Quad, …) chose the buttons
  and the workflow; now it chooses how the section is actually paired. Swiss
  styles pair a Swiss round; Round Robin / Double Round Robin / Quad pair an
  all-play-all schedule. The button you click and the pairings you get can no
  longer disagree.

- **Older imported events pair the way they read.** Some events imported from
  SwissSys / NA Chess Hub carried an internal "round robin" tag left over from
  the file format even though they're really Swiss. Those sections correctly
  showed **"Pair next round"** but, in v0.27, could still pair a full round
  robin if you clicked it. Now they pair a single Swiss round as shown — no
  surprises on legacy files.

- **The popped-out Pairings window matches your screen.** "Open in window" on
  the Pairings tab now shows the **same columns as your on-screen grid** — same
  order, the same show/hide choices, your custom column names, and the on-screen
  headers (e.g. **Rating**, **W #**). It previously mirrored the printed
  wall-posting sheet, which could look different from what you were working in.
  Change a column in **Advanced Options** and the popped-out window follows
  along, just like the docked grid.

- **Download:** [FreePair-win-x64-Setup.exe](https://github.com/freepairchess/FreePair/releases/latest/download/FreePair-win-x64-Setup.exe)
- **Portable zip:** [FreePair-win-x64-Portable.zip](https://github.com/freepairchess/FreePair/releases/latest/download/FreePair-win-x64-Portable.zip)
- **Auto-update:** Existing FreePair installs (v0.3 and later) pick up v0.28
  automatically. To update now, click "Check for updates" in the Settings tab.

The installer bundles the .NET 10 runtime, the BBP Pairings FIDE engine, and
the rules-reference document — no additional downloads or configuration
required.

## Known limitations (deliberate trade-offs, planned for later)

- **Double Round Robin pairs a single all-play-all cycle for now.** Choosing
  Double Round Robin gives you the round-robin schedule and the right workflow;
  pairing a genuine second cycle (everyone plays everyone again with reversed
  colours) is a planned follow-up.

- **Column choices are app-wide preferences.** The columns you pick (and any
  custom names) apply across your events on this computer, not per-event — so a
  layout you set up for one tournament is ready for the next. This is true for
  the on-screen grid, the popped-out window, and the printed sheet alike.

- **A handful of late-round colour and pairing choices remain at the TD's
  discretion.** Validated against a large real-world SwissSys corpus, FreePair
  reproduces the great majority of pairings exactly and matches 100% on many
  representative events. A small number of genuinely discretionary situations
  — where two pairings are equally valid under the rule book and SwissSys picks
  one by convention — are documented and left to the TD rather than forced to a
  single interpretation.
