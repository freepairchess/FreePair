# FreePair v0.23

A pairing-features release. You can now run **accelerated pairings** for
large Swiss events — on both USCF- and FIDE-rated sections — and limit
**same-team avoidance to the opening rounds**. Both controls live right on
the section's pairing tab and in the round-1 setup dialog, and both stay
adjustable until the rounds they affect are actually paired.

## What's new in v0.23

- **Accelerated pairings for big fields.** Turn on acceleration for a
  section and choose how many opening rounds it covers. For those rounds the
  top half of the field is given a temporary point boost so the strongest
  players meet each other sooner and a large field sorts itself out in fewer
  rounds. It works for both USCF-rated and FIDE-rated sections, and it's a
  per-section choice — accelerate the 300-player Open without touching the
  small side sections.

- **Same-team avoidance, now for the first N rounds.** Previously "avoid
  pairing teammates" was all-or-nothing for the whole event. You can now say
  "avoid teammates for the first 3 rounds" — common for scholastic and
  club-team events where you want teammates kept apart early but are happy to
  let the standings decide later. The round count is **inclusive** (3 means
  rounds 1–3), and the setup dialog spells that out as you change it.

- **One consistent place to set both.** Acceleration and team-avoidance now
  sit together on each section's **Pairings** tab and in the **round-1
  pairing dialog**, each as a simple checkbox that reveals a "for first N
  rounds" box when you turn it on. (Acceleration used to live on the Event
  tab — it's moved here so every pairing preference is in one spot.)

- **Adjustable until it's locked in.** You can still change how many rounds
  acceleration or team-avoidance covers for any round that **hasn't been
  paired yet** — extend the window, or shorten it down to the rounds already
  played, but never in a way that would rewrite a round you've already
  paired. The on/off switch itself is set before round 1, then becomes
  read-only. Turning acceleration off keeps your round count, so flipping it
  back on restores your previous setting.

- **Clear, round-aware preview.** The pre-commit pairing preview now tells
  you whether **this** round is accelerated and whether teammates are being
  avoided in it, so there's no guessing about which rules were in force for
  the pairings you're about to accept.

the rules-reference document — no additional downloads or configuration
required.

## Known limitations (deliberate trade-offs, planned for later)

- **Acceleration uses the standard top-half method.** FreePair gives the
  upper half of the field one fictitious point per accelerated round (USCF
  28R). Alternative acceleration schemes aren't exposed as separate options
  yet; the top-half method is the one the vast majority of events use.

- **Acceleration applies to the opening rounds only.** It is, by design, a
  start-of-event tool: once a round inside the accelerated window has been
  paired, that window is fixed for those rounds. You can still extend
  acceleration to cover further unpaired rounds.

- **Same-team avoidance remains a preference, not an absolute.** As before,
  FreePair keeps teammates apart when it can but will pair them rather than
  distort the standings among the leaders (USCF 28N1 plus-two method). The
  new setting controls *which rounds* that preference is applied in, not how
  strictly it overrides the standings.
