# FreePair v0.11.20260608

FreePair is a free, open-source desktop application for chess-tournament directors. It imports SwissSys 11 `.sjson` files, runs USCF Swiss and FIDE Dutch pairings (via FreePair's own USCF engine and the BBP Pairings FIDE engine, both bundled), and gives the TD end-to-end tools to pair, score, intervene, and publish reports.

This release adds a **new feature: one-click quads**. If you run quad events — the popular small-section format where players are grouped into fours and play a round-robin — this turns what used to be manual section-building into a single button. No change to any existing pairing behaviour.

## What's new in v0.11

- **New: "Pair as Quads" splits a section into quads automatically.** On the Pairings tab of any section you haven't started pairing yet, a new **Pair as Quads** button groups the players — by rating, highest four together, then the next four, and so on — into four-player quads and **pairs each one immediately**. A quad is a three-round round-robin (everyone plays everyone), so there's nothing to compute round by round: the boards and colours appear at once, and you just enter results.
- **Colours are assigned the standard quad way.** Within each quad every player gets as even a split as three games allow — two of one colour and one of the other — with the higher seeds tending to get the extra white, exactly like a printed quad card. You don't have to set anything.
- **Leftover players become a single mini-Swiss group.** When the number of players isn't an exact multiple of four, the remainder doesn't get stranded as a lone one-, two-, or three-player fragment. Instead the last full quad joins them to make one **five-, six-, or seven-player Swiss group** that plays a short Swiss. For example, a 23-player section becomes **four quads plus a seven-player Swiss group**. The quads pair instantly; you pair the Swiss group with the normal "Pair next round" button.
- **Your original section is kept, not destroyed.** Splitting into quads sets the original section aside (it's preserved and clearly marked) and creates the quad and Swiss sections next to it. If you change your mind, you can restore the original and remove the generated sections — you never have to re-enter the players.

## Why this matters

Quad events are everywhere at the club and scholastic level, but setting them up by hand — sorting by rating, cutting the field into fours, creating a section per quad, and pairing each one — is fiddly and error-prone. **Pair as Quads** does the whole thing in one click and gets the round-robin colours right automatically, so you can seed, split, and start a quad event in seconds.

## Known limitations (still planned for later releases)

- **Quads are formed by rating order.** The split seeds strictly by rating (top four, next four, …); there's no manual re-seeding of who lands in which quad yet. If you need a non-rating split, you can still build the sections by hand as before.
- **SwissSys fidelity is unchanged from v0.10.** The pairing engine matches the curated regression corpus on the same set of sections; this release adds a feature without shifting the fidelity numbers.
- **Cross-half colour interchange (USCF 29E5d) remains present but switched off**, pending a validated rule for when SwissSys takes an interchange versus tolerating a colour clash.
- **`28L3` forfeit-win-bye exclusion** and **`28L4` "unless all others have already had a bye" escape** remain partial — small bye-picker refinements scoped as their own future branches.
