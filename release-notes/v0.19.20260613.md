# FreePair v0.19

A scholastic-events release. FreePair now handles the USCF "avoid teammates"
preference the way the rule book intends — as a *preference*, not an absolute
ban — and explains the rule to you right where you turn it on. Upgrading is
recommended for any TD running combined individual-team or scholastic Swisses;
events that don't use team avoidance pair exactly as before.

## What's new in v0.19

- **Smarter "avoid same team" pairings (the USCF plus-two method).**
  Turning on team avoidance no longer forces teammates apart at any cost.
  FreePair now follows the USCF plus-two method:
  - Teammates are still kept apart whenever a score group **can** be paired
	that way — at every score level.
  - In the lower and middle of the field, if teammates can't be separated
	inside their group, one is floated into the neighbouring group to avoid
	the matchup — exactly as before.
  - **Among the leaders** (players two or more net wins above an even score),
	players are no longer pulled out of their score group just to dodge a
	teammate. If the only pairing left is teammate-vs-teammate, FreePair now
	**accepts** it rather than distorting the standings race. This protects
	the fairness of the prize contention at the top of the wall chart.

  A genuine rematch — two players who have already met — is **never** created
  by this change; only the same-team preference is relaxed, and only at the
  top. When the engine does accept a teammate pairing, the board is labelled
  **"Same-team pairing accepted"** in the "Why this pairing?" dialog so you
  can see it was a deliberate rule decision, not an oversight.

- **An explanation when you switch team avoidance on.** Checking "Avoid same
  team" — either on a section or in the round-1 setup dialog — now pops a
  short, plain-language explainer of the plus-two behaviour so you know what
  to expect before you rely on it. Every USCF rule number in that dialog is a
  clickable link that opens the matching rule in the bundled rules reference
  on GitHub, the same way the per-pairing "?" explanations already work. The
  "Avoid same team" tooltips were expanded to match.

## Known limitations (deliberate trade-offs, planned for later)

- **The plus-two threshold is fixed at the rule-book default.** USCF 28N3
  lets a TD set a different threshold and 28N2 adds a last-round exception;
  neither is exposed as a configurable option yet. Team avoidance uses the
  standard two-net-wins line.
- **Same-club avoidance still shares the same-team logic.** A separate
  club-only field and predicate remain future work; today the toggle treats
  clubs and teams alike.
- **Prior colour and board-ordering divergences from SwissSys** catalogued in
  v0.18 are unchanged; none change who plays whom or whether anyone meets
  twice.
