# FreePair v0.48

A **full team-event pairing** release. FreePair now pairs whole teams against
each other — round after round — and derives every team result automatically
from the board games you already record. If you run Olympiad-style team Swiss
events (or Bughouse team events), this is the release you've been waiting for.
Nothing changes for ordinary individual events, so upgrading is low-risk and
recommended.

## What's new in v0.48

### Full team pairing

- **Pair whole teams, round by round.** For a team section, **Pair Next Round**
  now pairs *teams* against each other and lays out the individual boards for
  each match automatically, with colours alternating board to board. You keep
  entering board results the way you always have; the team match score is worked
  out for you.
- **Team results are derived, never double-entered.** Match points, board
  points, byes, and the team standings are all computed from the individual
  board games. There's no separate team scoresheet to keep in sync — record the
  boards and the team picture updates itself.
- **Guided first-round setup.** The first team round opens a setup dialog where
  you confirm the **match size** (boards per match), the **bye policy**, the
  **team rating mode** (top boards or whole squad), and board-order enforcement,
  with plain-language explanations for each choice. Once the first round is
  paired these settings lock, so the event stays consistent.

### New team views

- **Team Roster tab.** See every team in seed order with its board-ordered
  line-up. Each team reads as a single block — the team name and totals show
  once — and the team-size cell is tinted by readiness (green = full squad,
  yellow = can field a match, red = short) so you can spot problems at a glance.
- **Team Standings tab.** An Olympiad-style crosstable ranked by match points,
  then board points, then team Sonneborn-Berger, with a column for every round.
  **Teams level on match points now share a place** (for example *1-3* or
  *4-11*) and, within that group, are ordered by the tiebreakers. A built-in
  "How teams are ranked" note explains the order.
- **Team Wall Chart tab.** A round-by-round wall chart for teams, matching the
  familiar individual wall chart.
- **Tidier tab order.** Individual-player tabs come first (Roster, Pairings,
  Standings, Wall Chart), then the team tabs (Team Roster, Team Pairing, Team
  Standings, Team Wall Chart), so the section reads top to bottom.

### Readiness and safety

- **Pre-flight readiness check.** Before pairing a team round, FreePair warns you
  about teams that can't field a full match, so you can fix line-ups before
  pairings go out.
- **Print any team view to PDF.** Team Roster, Team Standings, and Team Wall
  Chart all print to a clean landscape PDF with the usual event header and
  footer.

## Known limitations

- **Team byes follow FIDE draw-equivalent scoring by default.** A team that
  receives a full-round bye is scored as a drawn match by default (you can switch
  to match-win in the team setup). This matches the FIDE handbook default for
  team events.
- **SwissSys fidelity is unchanged.** The Swiss pairing engine and its regression
  coverage are exactly as in the previous version; the known divergences remain
  catalogued as deliberate future-work trade-offs.
